Surveyor 9

2-4 players, 20-40 Minutes
A quick card-based area control game on the surface of a new planet.

June, 2023 Josh Huf

Additional resources: 5 small discs and 10 small cubes in each players colour, 1 small disc in white as the satellite token, 30-40 coins or tokens for scoring.

Surveyor 9 is a short area control game where players have been deployed to map out a new planet. Each turn, place a card in one of the four zones of the planet and mark your work with one of your survey markers. Later on, you can spend your survey markers to deploy beacons, which may help you steal credit from your opponents. There is a timer though; the corporate satellite is orbiting the planet. Each time it passes one of the cardinal directions, it will check who has done the most work there (i.e, has the most beacons and survey markers) and will award credit to that player. Once the set number of rounds is up, the player with the most credit wins. This a spatial game; players will be able to progress faster by creating chains of matching terrain types. Points are also awarded for completing objective cards that sometimes require spatial reasoning. There is also a medium amount of player interaction; you can cut off your opponents terrain regions while you compete for credit.

Bower

2 players, 30-60 Minutes
A path-finding pick-up and deliver game for 2.

April, 2019 Josh Huf

Additional resources: 14 green tokens (2 large, 12 small), 14 white tokens (2 large, 12 small), 8 purple tokens, 12 six-sided dice, and 1 token of any size/colour.

Bower is a competitive 2 player, pick-up and deliver game with engine building upgrades and push-your-luck scoring elements. Gameplay revolves around strategic pathfinding and turn optimisation. On your turn, your opponent will first populate the forest with berries and trinkets using the seed deck. You may then alter the paths through the forest by rotating the 7 central tiles, before moving your Bowerbird along its current path, collecting the trinkets you pass over. If you can return your bowerbird to the nest, you score points depending on the number of trinkets it returned with. You also earn a powerful crest to enhance your future turns. The player with the most points from their bower and personal goal cards is the winner.

The Captain's Parrot

2-4 players, 2 Minutes
A frantic card drafting game you can play standing up.

October, 2019 Josh Huf

Additional resources: None.

The Captain's Parrot is a (less than) 5 minute competitive card drafting game that tests players quick processing skills. It was designed for one of the Button Shy 18 card design challenges. In the game, players are deck-hands on a pirate ship, loading chests full of loot under the watchful eye of the Captain's Parrot. Each round, players will choose a card from their hand to keep, passing the rest to the player on their left. The cards have both a varying number of coloured gems, as well as a unique end-game scoring requirement (such as having the most rubies, or having an even number of pearls). However, one of the players will have the Parrot card, and has to immediately start counting down from 10. When they reach 0, any player who has not made a choice is spotted by the parrot, and must flip one of their cards upside down as a penalty, 'dropping' the treasure chest in their panic. It is a very quick, somewhat frantic game as players are making snap calculations, assessing their cards and trying to make the most of their scoring opportunities.

Tumblebee

1-100 players, 20 Minutes
Roll and write combo building and strategic movement.

May, 2019 Josh Huf

Additional resources: 2 six-sided dice, and a writing implement for each player.

Tumblebee is a 20 minute solo/multiplayer competitive puzzle. 2 dice are rolled, and players choose one result to use as a movement, and the other becomes a pollination value. Players move their bee around the board, attempting to encircle spaces with fully pollenated flowers to score points. The more your bee has moved, the more points a space is worth, but you can never return to a space you have previously visited. Because of this, the strategy for scoring big points is to maneuver around, pollinating all the flowers around a particular space and only then landing inside of it once you are certain you can pollinate all adjacent flowers.

  • Download print & play on Itch.io
  • Play on Tabletop Simulator

Development notes: I conceived this one back in 2019 when 'roll and write' games were really in their heyday. The game hasn't changed much from the original concept, however at one point it became a flip & fill; using a shuffled deck of cards to produce the movement and pollination values rather than dice rolls. This was trying to mitigate some of the bad luck of rolling consistently low numbers, which can be frustrating when trying to pollinate the higher petal count flowers. Ultimately, the tactility and accessibility of dice won out. Cards are harder to print, and during play tests I found it to be not quite as exciting. It did introduce a new mechanic of the spiders, however, which I kept in the final version of the game.
